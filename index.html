Surya Siddhanta and Modern Satellite Systems

From ancient Indian astronomy to modern satellite technology — exploring how early celestial calculations inspired humanity’s journey into space.

What is Surya Siddhanta?

Surya Siddhanta is an ancient Indian astronomical text written around 400–500 CE. It contains calculations about planetary motion, eclipses, trigonometry, and time measurement.

Ancient Observatories

Jantar Mantar and Ujjain Observatory were important centers for studying celestial movements and tracking time.

Modern Satellites

Satellites help in GPS, communication, weather forecasting, disaster monitoring, and space exploration.

Connection Between Both

Ancient astronomers observed the skies manually. Modern scientists use satellites and computers— but both aim to understand the universe.
